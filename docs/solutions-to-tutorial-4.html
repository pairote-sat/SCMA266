  1. To examine whether the cashflows are equivalent, we compare their present values.

    1. The present value of single payment of amount 14,802.44 at year 10 is \[ PV(0) = \frac{14,802.44}{1.04^{10}} = 10000.\]

    2. The present value of the level annuity of 400 payable yearly in arrears for the next 10 years plus a lump sum of 10,000 is \[ PV(0) = 400 a^{0.04}_{10} +\frac{10000}{1.04^{10}} = 10000.\]

    3. The present value of the level annuity of 1,232.91 payable yearly in arrears for the next 10 years. \[ PV(0) = 1,232.91 a^{0.04}_{10} = 10000.\]

It follows that the values of these cashflows are the same, i.e. equivalent.

  2. Working in time unit of half year, the equation of value is \[ f(i) = -5000(1+i)^{12 \times 2} + 300 s^i_{24} = 0. \] The yield \(i\) per half year is \(i = 3.1491266\%\) and hence the annual yield is \(6.397423\%.\)

  3. The equation of value is \[ f(i) = -100(1+i)^{7} + 20 s^i_{3}(1+i)^{4} + 25 s^i_{4} = 0. \] The annual yield is \(12.6209232\%.\)

  4. You are suggested to draw the time line for these cashflows. The equation of value is \[ f(i) = -5 \ddot{s}^i_{3}(1+i)^{9} + 4 s^i_{6} = 0. \] The annual yield is \(5.7285486\%.\)
